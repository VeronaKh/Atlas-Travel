Planning Your Trip

Citizens of the EU (Germany, Austria, Switzerland, France, etc.), UK, USA, Canada and many other countries do not need a visa for stays up to 90 days. A valid passport is sufficient. We always send you a current entry requirements update before your trip. If you're from a country that does require a visa, we can advise you on the process.

The most pleasant months are March–May and September–November. Temperatures are warm but manageable, wildflowers bloom in the valleys, and the light is extraordinary. Summer (June–August) is very hot in the Sahara — up to 48°C — and not ideal for desert trips. Winter is beautiful in the north and cities, but mountain treks may be affected by snow above 2500 m.

Practical Information

The Moroccan Dirham (MAD). It's not freely convertible outside Morocco, so exchange money on arrival. ATMs are widely available in cities. In the Sahara and small mountain villages, cash is essential — card payments are rare. We recommend bringing €200–300 in cash for personal spending on a 7-day trip.

Morocco is generally a safe travel destination. Millions of tourists — including many solo women — visit every year without issue. As in any destination, normal city awareness applies: watch your belongings in busy souks, use registered taxis, and dress modestly in rural areas. All our guides are vetted and experienced, and we're reachable by phone 24/7 during your trip.

No mandatory vaccinations are required for most nationalities. We recommend ensuring your routine vaccinations are up to date (tetanus, hepatitis A). Consult your doctor or a travel clinic 4–6 weeks before departure for personalised advice. We also recommend travel insurance that covers medical evacuation.

Morocco has four main languages: Darija (Moroccan Arabic), Tamazight (Berber), French and Spanish (in the north). Our guides speak English, German, French and Arabic. In tourist areas, English is widely understood. Our team communicates in English, German and French.

Sahara & Desert Trips

It's magical. Our desert camp near Merzouga has traditional Berber-style tents with proper beds, blankets and lanterns — not just a sleeping bag on sand. Evenings involve dinner around a campfire, Berber drumming, and an astonishing sky full of stars with zero light pollution. Mornings start with sunrise on the dunes — one of the most beautiful sights on Earth.

Very little fitness is required. The camel ride to the camp is typically 45–90 minutes and is manageable for almost everyone. Camels are gentle and our handlers walk alongside. If you prefer, you can drive to the camp and just ride the camels for a short sunset photo ride instead.

Essentials: high-factor sunscreen, a hat, sunglasses, a light scarf (for wind and sand), comfortable walking shoes, and layers for the cold nights — even in summer, desert temperatures drop dramatically after sunset. We provide a full packing list when you confirm your trip.

Atlas Mountain Trekking

We offer treks for all levels. Our introductory routes (Imlil Valley, Ourika Valley) are suitable for reasonably fit beginners — 3–5 hours walking per day on well-marked paths. Our Toubkal approach and multi-day routes are more demanding and require prior hiking experience and good physical fitness. We always match the route to your group's level.

On most treks we stay in Berber family gîtes (mountain guest houses) — simple, clean rooms with home-cooked meals. This is one of the highlights of Atlas trekking: eating tagine at a family table, learning a few words of Tamazight, and experiencing the genuine warmth of mountain hospitality. On the Toubkal summit approach, we use a mountain refuge.

Good walking boots are essential — no trainers. A daypack, trekking poles (we can lend these), waterproof jacket, sun protection and warm layers for evenings. Mules carry the main luggage between stages, so you only carry a light daypack on the trail. A full equipment list is sent on booking.

Booking & Payment

All our prices include: accommodation (riads or Berber camp), professional English-speaking guide, private transport during the trip, all activities listed in the itinerary, and daily breakfast. Lunches and dinners are included on trekking and desert trips. International flights are not included, but we're happy to advise on the best routes and times.

Send us an enquiry via the contact page or by email. We'll send a personalised proposal within 24 hours. To confirm a booking we require a 30% deposit, with the balance due 6 weeks before departure. We accept bank transfer, PayPal and major credit cards.

Cancellations more than 60 days before departure: full refund minus a €50 admin fee. 30–60 days: 50% refund. Less than 30 days: no refund. We strongly recommend taking out travel insurance that covers trip cancellation — we can suggest providers. In the rare case we must cancel a trip due to safety reasons, you receive a full refund.

We don't sell insurance directly, but we strongly recommend it for all our trips. A good policy should cover: trip cancellation, medical expenses, and emergency evacuation. We recommend checking providers like World Nomads, Allianz Travel or your own bank's travel coverage. Ask us and we'll point you to what other travellers have used.
